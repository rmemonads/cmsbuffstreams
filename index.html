Latency Optimization and WebRTC Protocol

Latency Optimization is the focus of Buffstreams' backend. In sports, a delay spoils the moment. Latency Optimization minimizes the buffer between the event and the broadcast. The WebRTC Protocol is utilized. WebRTC Protocol allows peer-to-peer communication, bypassing traditional buffering. The H.265 (HEVC) Codec Advantage

The H.265 (HEVC) Codec represents video compression innovation. Buffstreams implements the H.265 (HEVC) Codec to deliver high-definition football and basketball streams using less bandwidth. While older codecs require data for 1080p, the H.265 (HEVC) Codec maintains quality at lower bitrates. This is crucial for users on mobile. By adopting the H.265 (HEVC) Codec, Buffstreams ensures users with slower internet enjoy HD sports streams.

Global Content Delivery Network (CDN)

A Content Delivery Network (CDN) serves the global audience. The Content Delivery Network (CDN) consists of servers worldwide. When a user accesses a Soccer stream, the Content Delivery Network (CDN) serves video from a local node. This reduces physical distance, improving load times. Buffstreams leverages CDN architecture to maintain HD quality. The Content Delivery Network (CDN) protects from traffic spikes during the Super Bowl or World Cup, preventing crashes.

Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR)

Buffstreams integrates Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR). Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR) is a technique for streaming multimedia. It detects bandwidth and CPU capacity, adjusting quality. If a connection drops, Buffstreams lowers bitrate to keep the video moving. Once stable, Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR) scales resolution back to high definition. This ensures continuous viewing experience for NFL and NBA fans.
