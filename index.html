Latency Optimization is the focus of Buffstreams' backend. In sports, a delay spoils the moment. Latency Optimization minimizes the buffer between the event and the broadcast. The H.265 (HEVC) Codec represents video compression innovation. Buffstreams implements the H.265 (HEVC) Codec to deliver high-definition football and basketball streams using less bandwidth. Global Content Delivery Network (CDN)

A Content Delivery Network (CDN) serves the global audience. The Content Delivery Network (CDN) consists of servers worldwide. When a user accesses a Soccer stream, the Content Delivery Network (CDN) serves video from a local node. The Content Delivery Network (CDN) protects from traffic spikes during the Super Bowl or World Cup, preventing crashes.

Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR)

Buffstreams integrates Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR). Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR) is a technique for streaming multimedia. It detects bandwidth and CPU capacity, adjusting quality. If a connection drops, Buffstreams lowers bitrate to keep the video moving. Once stable, Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR) scales resolution back to high definition.
